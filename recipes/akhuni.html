Axone (Akhuni)

Description

Axone is known for its strong, pungent, and deeply savory umami flavor. The fermentation process creates a unique taste that's often described as cheesy, earthy, and intensely aromatic. It's used in small amounts to add depth and complexity to dishes

Axone is typically prepared by fermenting soybeans, resulting in a dark, sticky paste with a powerful aroma. It's used as a seasoning agent, adding a distinctive flavor to stews, curries, and other traditional Naga and some Manipuri preparations.

Ingredients

  • Soybeans
  • Water (for soaking and boiling)
  • Banana leaves (or other suitable leaves for wrapping)
  • Ash or similar (optional, for creating a warm environment)
  • Time (for fermentation)

STEPS

  1. Soak the Soybeans: Soak the soybeans in water for several hours or overnight until they are softened.
  2. Boil the Soybeans: Boil the soaked soybeans until they are very tender and easily crushed.
  3. Crush the Soybeans: Drain the boiled soybeans and crush them into a coarse paste.
  4. Wrap the Paste: Wrap the crushed soybean paste tightly in banana leaves or other suitable leaves.
  5. Ferment: Place the wrapped soybean paste in a warm, dark place for several days (the duration varies depending on the desired level of fermentation). Some people use ash to create a warm environment.
  6. Check Fermentation: Regularly check the paste for the desired level of fermentation, indicated by its aroma and texture.
  7. Store or Use: Once fermented, the Axone can be stored for later use or used immediately in cooking.
